Spaghetti Carbonara

Description:

A delectable, Italian pasta dish comprised of spaghetti and a egg,cheese-based sauce. Recently Spaghetti Carbonara has been increasing in popularity in the United States, and with this, the emergence of quasi-Italian chefs who heavily scrutinize those who do not produce this dish in their so-called "Italian-authentic" way. Follow this recipe to ensure you do not fall under the hateful gaze of these critics.

Ingredients:

Yields 4 Servings

  • Salt
  • 2 large eggs and 2 large egg yolks
  • 1 ounce grated, pecorino Romano cheese
  • 1 ounce grated, Parmesean cheese
  • Coarsely ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3.5 ounces guanciale, sliced into 1/4 inch think by 1/3 inch sqaure pieces
  • 12 ounces spaghetti

Steps:

  1. Place a large pot of slightly salted water over high heat and let boil. Fill a large bowl of warm water and set aside.
  2. In a mixing bowl, mix together eggs, yolks, pecorino, and Parmesean. Season with a pinch of salt and generous amount of pepper.
  3. While water is boiling, heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat, add guanciale, and let cook until just crispy. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. Add spaghetti to boiling water and cook just before al dente. Just before pasta is done, reheat guanciale in a pan if needed. Set aside 1 cup of pasta water before draining pasta and adding it to skillet over low heat. Stir for one minute.
  5. Empty large bowl of water, dry it, then add pasta-guanciale mixture. Stir in cheese mixture and add pasta water to ehance creaminess if needed. Serve immediately, topping with additional pecorino and pepper to your liking. Enjoy!
